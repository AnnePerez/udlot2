Udlot-udlot by Jose Maceda

Philippine National Artist for Music


Performance Notes

Performance duration: ~10 minutes

Please read prior to performance:

  1. The score can be accessed at http://www.astermux.com/udlot2/udlotudlot.html. For offline access, please go https://github.com/AnnePerez/udlot2. Download the whole folder as a zip file. Unzip the folder and click on the udlotudlot.html file.

  2. Select your assigned group (DRONE, GROUP 1, GROUP2, VOICE1, VOICE 2 OR VOICE3), then click the "Start Performance" button. If you want to restart your performance, click RESTART or reload the page. If you click the wrong group, reload the page.

  3. Make sure that your recording includes the initial bell in the count down. Mute the audio (or for VOICES, insert your earphones to your device so you can hear the audio guide) after the initial bell when you record your performance.

    Use the highest resolution possible for your video recording. Make sure to have an adequate amount of light in your video recording. If you are recording using your mobile phone, use landscape orientation with the instruments visible, as long as the audio is clear and audible.

    Find a quiet spot to record your video performance. Minimize ambient noises by turning off loud appliances such as electric fans to reduce noise in your recording.

  4. While this performance is virtual, please wear appropriate performance attire.

  5. Please submit your recorded performance/performances to udlotudlot2020@gmail.com. Please use this format for saving your file: UDLOTGroupFullName (ex. UDLOTVoice1KatzTrangco). Each performance should be saved as a different file

    The deadline for submission is: 15 May 2020
    Please send your inquiries to udlotudlot2020@gmail.com.

DRONE

Beat a pair of percussion sticks* continuously until the end of the piece. Follow the tempo of the score.

* Percussion sticks can be wooden sticks, claves, or household implements that produce a flat (non-resonant) sound such as wooden utensils on the bottom of a pan.

MIXED INSTRUMENTS: GROUP 1 and GROUP 2

Prepare the three instruments you will need during the performance: a whistle*, a buzzer** and a pair of bamboo tubes***, and keep them nearby.

* Alternatives to the bamboo whistle/flute can be used such as the panpipe (use one pitch only) or the mouth of an open bottle.

** Alternatives to buzzers can be the Jewâ€™s harp / jaw harp / kubing or any implement that can simulate a buzzing sound such as an egg whisk.

*** If bamboo tubes are not available, you can use household implements such hitting a fork against the side of a plate to produce a resonant sound.

For the duration of each cell number, use the instrument indicated in the instruction box and choose any counting pair displayed in the instruction box. Within one cell, you can play any combination of counting pairs.

Play the instrument twice within a counting pair: one at the end of the count of the 1st number, and another at the end of the count of the 2nd number. For example, for 2 & 4, count 1 2 - 1 2 3 4 (play at 2 and 4).

Each player can choose their own tempo.
